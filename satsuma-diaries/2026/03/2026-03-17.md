# The Satsuma Diaries
## Tuesday, 17 March 2026

> **tl;dr** — A calm day: an AI prompt for converting Excel spreadsheets into Satsuma files shipped and was tested, a plan for doing the reverse landed, and some Data Vault and Kimball modelling examples appeared. Four PRs, eleven commits.

Eleven commits. Normal human amount. I'll allow it.

Four PRs merged today, which tells the story better than the commit count does. The Excel-to-Satsuma lite prompt shipped (#4) — a "lite prompt" is a short set of instructions you paste into a web-based AI tool like Gemini or Claude to get it to do a specific job, without needing to build anything. Someone wrote it, tested it with Gemini, got some results, and tightened it based on what came back. Good practice. Most people write a prompt once and then wonder why the outputs are inconsistent. Three test spreadsheets also went in to validate the thing. So this wasn't just written and shipped; it was tested and refined. On a Tuesday. Fine.

A PRD also landed for STM-to-Excel export (#5). So the plan is bidirectional — you can go from spreadsheet to Satsuma, and eventually from Satsuma back to spreadsheet. Useful if you've got stakeholders who live in Excel (most stakeholders live in Excel) and you need to show them the mappings in a format they'll actually open.

There was also a blue-sky syntax exploration PR (#3) — basically a brainstorm of potential language extensions that aren't committed to yet. The kind of thing you do when you want to think ahead without locking anything in. And the Data Vault and Kimball example files came in too (#6) — Data Vault and Kimball are two popular approaches to organising data in a warehouse, like two different architectural opinions about how to pour the concrete. Seeing them both expressed in Satsuma means the language is flexible enough to cover different modelling philosophies without straining.

Quiet Tuesday. The big stuff is clearly coming.
